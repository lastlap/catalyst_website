Why Not Give a Shot at CA

15/6/20212 min readAlbert Denny

Chartered Accountancy (CA) is a prestigious professional qualification in the field of Accountancy, Taxation, Finance and Audit, offered by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI). To become a CA, one needs to clear three levels of examination namely Foundation, Intermediate and Final along with three years of practical training (also known as articleship). A successful candidate will then become a member of ICAI and will be addressed with the prefix CA (it sticks with you for the rest of your life!).

Sounds very simple. Doesn’t it? You might be thinking “Yeah I got this, easy peasy”

Hold On Tiger! This is where things get pretty interesting.

You will be amazed to know that in a country of over 130 crore people, we only have over 3 lakh CAs. That is 0.02% of the total population.

DAMN! WHY SO?

Well is it because nobody takes up CA?

Nope! It’s because not many end up clearing CA exams.

CA is considered as one of the toughest course in India. The pass percentages of the different levels average around 10-15%. (I am being very liberal with these numbers, sometimes it even goes down to 4-5%).

So I am not here persuade you to take up CA. Just to give you a few pointers why CA might be a good career choice for you.

  1. MONEY AND RESPECT - Obviously there is a lot of money to be made but more importantly CA’s command a lot of respect in the Indian economic diaspora. Companies pay top dollars to get opinions of CAs on how to turnaround their businesses.
  2. JOB PROSPECTS - All industries and organization require CAs for its accounting department. The job prospect of this field is very much brighter. As a CA, you can work in government or public service undertakings as well as the private sector companies. You can also have an option to either be employed with a firm as an employee or you can choose to open your own private practice. (The word “Recession” doesn’t exist in the dictionary of a CA.)
  3. COST EFFECTIVE -Pursuing Chartered Accountancy is not an expensive affair at all. The estimated total cost to complete the course along with classes come to around Rs 3 lakhs. What makes it even more lucrative is that fact that you even end up earning money during your practical training of 3 years. (That is a win-win). Lower Cost coupled with higher salary prospects provides this course with a higher ROI (Return on Investment) than many other degrees in our country.
  4. YOU GET PAID FOR YOUR SIGNATURE - All public and private companies in India require their Financial Statements to be audited by CAs. This has been mandated by law. So by becoming a CA and getting the power to sign Financial Statement and Audit Reports, you become an ambassador of the Government in ensuring Financial Transparency in the economy.
  5. TAX PLANNING - India is growing into a super economy and along with that India is going to see a rise in the level of income of its people. This means there is going to be a lot of folks, who would want someone to help them with saving taxes. Being a CA gives you the knowledge and expertise to help people in their tax planning and ensuring their compliance with the Income tax laws of the country.

As it is rightly said that, CA is not a tough course. It is a course for tough people. And of course it is hard. It is supposed to be hard. If it was easy, everyone would do it. Hard is what makes it great.
