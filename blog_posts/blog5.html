Breaking out of our shell

16/6/20212 min readAkanksha Singh

I had completed my senior secondary schooling from Indian School Al Seeb and the disparity between the level of pressure among the students in India and Oman is something so fascinating yet at times scary to experience.

However I consider myself lucky that I got to complete my couple of school abiding years in Oman but it came with certain shortcomings i.e., I always felt we were not given enough career guidance and that is why I was so happy to work with Sapna who was and has always been a very good friend of mine since high school. At times we get lost with the number of opportunities the professional world has to offer and we don't realize what we are really putting ourselves into , even when we take simple decisions like choosing our stream after 10th class.

Proper guidance can not only help a student be motivated for the goal but it also builds intrests in the base knowledge of their stream which is very important.

I used to hate programming when I was in 11th standard but sadly the only optional subjects My school offered was a programming language and biology and clearly I did not want to choose the latter because that would have been very troublesome for me.

I always used to wonder how do people even program all these big softwares, programming was so boring, it didn't make sense and what not...but now its different, I chose Computer science engineering(specialized in Artificial intelligence and Data Science) as my UG and I am actively learning so many things on a daily basis, I had this myth that computer science engineering means i need to be pro efficient at programming but only to realize later that , I do have a lot more options than that, I can be learning any programming language and its not compulsory for me to learn what Subject I had in school and all these programming languages are so different amongst them.

If someone asked 12th grade Akansha if she would choose Computer Science Engineering she would have never agreed, but look at me now, entering my 3rd year of college with so much excitement and so much willing to learn more and more about my field of work each and every day. Putting into simpler terms, a little bit of guidance can change our perception of what is "tough" and we here at catalyst with an excellent team will do our best to provide you with the best guidance.
